Universal Construction Company Website Plan

Core concept

Create a modular website and business platform called BuildHub: a professional public website combined with tools that help construction companies generate leads, manage projects, communicate with clients, and reduce administrative work.

It should work for general contractors, residential builders, commercial contractors, remodelers, specialty trades, small local companies, and large multi-location firms. The system must be configurable rather than designed around one niche.

Problems it should solve

- Weak or outdated websites
- Inconsistent lead generation and slow quote requests
- Poor client communication and scattered project information
- Missed deadlines and follow-ups
- Difficulty showing past work
- Manual paperwork and unclear project status
- Lack of reviews, referrals, and marketing visibility

Main website experience

1. A conversion-focused homepage communicating what the company builds, where it operates, its specialties, proof of quality, and clear calls to action: request an estimate, schedule a consultation, view projects, call, and track an existing project.
2. Reusable service pages with description, project types, process, images, ranges, FAQs, testimonials, service areas, and estimate requests.
3. A filterable project portfolio with photos, location, work type, scope, timeline, materials, challenges, result, and customer testimonial.
4. An intelligent, guided estimate request covering project type, location, size, timeline, budget, uploads, and contact information.
5. Localized service-area pages for each city or region served.

Client portal

Clients can view project status and milestones, review documents, approve selections, upload files, message the company, view invoices and payments, review change orders, see progress photos, and request support.

Contractor dashboard

Provide a lead inbox and qualification, estimate tracking, customer database, project pipeline, tasks, calendar, documents, communications, review requests, website editing, and analytics. Keep it simple for small contractors while allowing advanced modules for larger companies.

Universal design system

Use a shared, configurable foundation for logo, colors, fonts, services, project categories, service regions, team members, testimonials, calls to action, forms, and portal features.

Possible modes: simple business website, lead-generation website, portfolio-focused website, client portal website, and full construction operations platform.

Recommended MVP

- Responsive company website
- Service pages
- Project portfolio
- Guided estimate request
- Testimonials and reviews
- Service-area pages
- Lead management dashboard
- Basic client project portal
- Website editor
- Basic analytics

Avoid accounting, payroll, advanced scheduling, or complex bidding in the first release.

Customer workflow

Discovery -> services and projects -> guided estimate -> qualification and contact -> proposal -> client portal -> organized updates/files/payments/messages -> review and referral request.

Revenue model

- Starter: website, services, portfolio, contact forms
- Professional: lead management, estimates, reviews, analytics
- Growth: client portal, documents, payments, team access
- Enterprise: multiple locations, permissions, integrations, custom branding

Optional setup fees, SEO, content/photography, ad management, integrations, and payment transaction fees.

Roadmap

1. Foundation: research, universal data structure, design system, website templates, estimate flow.
2. Lead generation: dashboard, automated responses, CRM pipeline, scheduling, reviews, local SEO.
3. Client experience: portal, updates, documents, approvals, change orders, progress photos.
4. Operations: teams, tasks, scheduling, inventory, vendors, accounting integrations.
5. Intelligence: lead scoring, follow-ups, timeline estimation, communication assistance, business insights.

Success metrics

Track estimate requests, conversion and qualification rates, response time, proposal acceptance, portal usage, reduced repetitive calls, review completion, satisfaction, recurring revenue, and retention.

Most important principle

The platform should be simple by default and powerful when needed. A small remodeler should be able to launch a polished website in one afternoon, while a larger company can activate portals, teams, locations, approvals, integrations, and advanced reporting without changing platforms.
